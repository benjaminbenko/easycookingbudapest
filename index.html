Kőrözött

Quick and easy spiced-cheese dip, often served as an appetizer with chunks of bread or spread on pepper slices.It can be made of cow's cottage cheese or for a stronger and more traditional taste with sheep's cottage cheese. Great finger food to acompany a pálinka-party!

Want to taste? Book now!
Pogácsa

These bite-sized savory dough balls are Hungary's favourite snack. They can be made with cheese, pumpkin seeds, bacon, cabbage, potato, whatever you like. They are served at parties, weddings, birthday celebrations, get-togethers, so basically, where there are Hungarians, there's also pogácsa!

Want to taste? Book now!
Borsóleves

This is a simple dish that can be made in less then 30 minutes. The nokedli, or traditional Hungarian dumplings, add body to the soup without weighing down the delicious blend of sauteed onion and authentic Hungarian paprika.

Want to taste? Book now!
Gulyásleves

The number one, most famous Hungarian dish. It originates from the countryside and was the main dish of the herdsmen looking after cattle, the name gulyás refers to this profession. At first it was a dish only for the peasants, but slowly worked its way to aristocracy. It is a heavy, filling soup, originally made with beef, but can also be prepared with pork.

Want to taste? Book now!
Paprikás Csirke

Paprika, being the main spice of most Hungarian dishes, gives its name, colour and taste to this great chicken dish. Here, the meat is boiled, instead of fried, also something typical in Hungarian cuisine. Not hard to prepare but very special if made right!

Want to taste? Book now!
Rántott Sajt

Fried cheese. Is there anything else you need to know? :) Great traditional dish for vegetarians!

Want to taste? Book now!
Mákosguba

A bread pudding with poppy seads. Hungarian desserts are very often made with delicious poppy seeds, if you try this one, you'll know why! There are many variations to this dessert, but it can not be missed from any holiday table.

Want to taste? Book now!
Túrógombóc

Sweetened cottage cheese boiled, rolled in toasted bread crumbs, and served with sour cream and sugar. Heavenly and very filling!

Want to taste? Book now!

Classes

Learn here and cook anywhere

Learn how to cook Hungary's world-famous dishes with ingredients that you can buy anywhere around the globe!

Amazing culinary experience

Shop with us in one of our historic and traditional markets and then join us cooking our favourite dishes. We are a boutique cooking school with an emphasis on creating a friendly workshop atmosphere in smaller groups and providing a hands-on cooking experience. Learn how to easily prepare real Hungarian home food - also for cooking beginners!

Taste traditional drinks

Try our Pálinka (fruit brandy) and some of our great wines. If you would prefer something non-alcoholic, try our home-made elderberry syrup!

Learn about Hungary

Trace the roots of Hungarian cuisine back in time through different regions, learn a lot about Hungarian history, social life and traditions. Find out what not to miss, where to dine during your stay in our small, but culturally very diverse country!

Right in the city center

Meeting point from Monday to Saturday is at the Great Market Hall main entrance (1093 Budapest, Vámház krt. 1-3.) and on Sunday at Szimpla Kert entrance (Kazinczy u. 14, 1075)

Alle unsere Kurse sind auch in deutscher Sprache möglich. Bitte kontaktieren Sie uns für mehr Information.

Class Schedule
1

Market visit

Duration: approx. 1 hour

Meeting point is at the entrance of the market. Once everyone arrived, we will head into the market to shop for our ingredients and also extras if you want to take something home, I can help you choose from our national specialties.

2

To the Kitchen!

Duration: approx. 10 minutes

Then we'll take a taxi from the market to the kitchen.

3

Tasting Spirit & Introduction

Duration: approx. 15 minutes

Once at the kitchen, you can taste our world famous Pálinka (fruit brandy) or elderberry syrup if you would prefer something non alcoholic. Meanwhile I will give you a short, interactive presentation about the history of Hungarian cuisine.

4

Let's start cooking!

Duration: approx. 3 hours

And finally! Let's start cooking! We will prepare 4 traditional courses: a starter, soup, main course and dessert. We are going to cook together and I'll tell you all about our traditions, while you can enjoy a bottle of our great wines. One bottle for two guests is our gift to you. Last but not least, let’s enjoy the fruits of our labor: let's have a great meal together!

Our Story

Cecilia

I am a food enthusiast born and raised in Hungary. Although my original studies (veterinary medicine) are far away from the food industry, culinary pleasures always had an important role in my and my family's life. Both of my grandmothers are from the countryside, but from opposite sides of the country, so I learnt different ways of cooking our traditional dishes very early on in my life. Cooking and talking about our traditions, culture are not a job for me, but a great way to spend my free time. Also loving travel and getting to know other cultures, an additional benefit of this course for me is meeting wonderful, open minded people from all around the world! So please join me on one of my cooking sessions!

Tünde

I was always impressed by international gastronomy, especially when I was traveling abroad. Tasting unknown foods and flavours means getting to know a peace of the culture, which influences the whole created picture of the destination and its residentials. I’m a Hungarian food lover since my aunts from the countryside showed me the traditional ways of food preparation. Despite my original studies (economics) taking me in a different direction for a while, finally I’m back to show you the riches of the Hungarian gastronomy. So please join me to enjoy and learn secrets about our unique food culture.
